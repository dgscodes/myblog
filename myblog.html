Walk a mile with his face

Written by: Doron Simon

Dear readers,

I've recently just read the book 'Wonder' by R. J. Palacio, a book documenting the life of Auggie Pullman, a kid born with a genetic disease that altered the look of his face, resulting in a multitude of surgies which still did not fix the appearance of his face. 'Wonder' is full of many life lessons that we can all inculcate in our lives. The idea of sensitivity, kindness, patience, selflessness and tolerance are all very prevalent and so too very important. But the idea I would like to focus on is the idea of perepective.

My precepts

Month Precept Interpretation
September 'When given the choice between being right or being kind, choose kind'

- Dr. Wayne W. Dryer		 Letting go is sometimes necessary, even if you know you're right, and it's fair to do to another as they've done to you - sometimes being kind is the better option, even if you're not doing what's considered fair or right.
October 'Your deeds are your monuments

-inscription on an Egyptian tomb		 What you do, your acts of kindness, your contibution to society, is your legacy which is on display as is a statue - representing the kind of person you are, a legacy which lasts for decades. You will die without any posessions, but be remembered for your deeds.
December Audentes fortuna iuvat (fortune favours the bold)

- Virgil		 You have to be bold in order to be 'lucky'. To be successful means to take risks, to be willing to fail.
June 'Just follow the day and reach for the sun'

- The Polyphonic Spree		 Follow through on your dreams, even through the toughest of days, in order to make your dream a reality. Just go with the flow, and make the best of adversities because those challenges make the dream even more rewarding once accomplished.
